# Summary Report: “Event Meet Up”

### Event Information

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| **Event name** | Event Meet Up |
| **Time** | 09:00, May 30, 2026 |
| **Location** | 26th Floor, Bitexco Tower, 02 Hai Trieu Street, Saigon Ward, Ho Chi Minh City |
| **Role** | Participant |

### Event Objectives

- Create a space for networking and learning among AWS/FCJ community members.
- Share practical experiences in AWS learning, hackathons, teamwork, and technology product development.
- Provide new perspectives on DevOps mindset, cloud cost awareness, confidence, and AI applications in real products.

### Speakers and Topics

- **Huynh Thai Linh** - What Beginners Worry About in AWS.
- **The Ballers** - Hackathon experience.
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** - Why We Always Need Confidence.
- **Tran Minh Quan** - The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster.
- **Khac Uy** - A Familiar Enemy.
- **Tran Huu Nghia** - Introduction to an AI-based numerology/fortune-telling website.

### Main Content

#### Huynh Thai Linh - What Beginners Worry About in AWS

This presentation focused on common concerns of AWS beginners, especially cloud costs, forgotten resources, and unexpected charges. It helped me understand that cloud practice requires not only technical skills, but also the habit of checking Billing, monitoring resources, and cleaning up after each lab.

#### The Ballers - Hackathon Experience

The Ballers shared real hackathon experiences, including time pressure, unexpected bugs, continuous teamwork, and completing a product within a limited timeframe. The presentation showed that a hackathon is not only about ideas, but also about collaboration, problem-solving, and product presentation.

#### Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu - Why We Always Need Confidence

Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu discussed the importance of confidence in study, work, and student life. The talk emphasized that confidence helps people express ideas, try new things, communicate proactively, and develop themselves more effectively.

#### Tran Minh Quan - The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

Tran Minh Quan presented the hidden work behind a project, such as deployment, environment configuration, monitoring, security, logging, and incident preparation. From this session, I learned that DevOps is not only about deploying code, but also about preparing the system to run reliably and recover from issues.

#### Khac Uy - A Familiar Enemy

Khac Uy shared about procrastination, a familiar problem in study and work. The talk reminded me to manage time better, prioritize important tasks, and start earlier instead of waiting until the deadline.

#### Tran Huu Nghia - AI Website Introduction

The final speaker introduced an AI-based website that supports numerology/fortune-telling interpretation. Although there was no separate slide deck, the content was still interesting because it showed how AI can be integrated into a website to personalize content, analyze user input, and create interactive experiences.

### Key Takeaways

- AWS learning requires both technical skills and cloud cost awareness.
- Effective teamwork depends on clear task allocation, communication, and fast problem-solving.
- Confidence helps learners become more proactive in sharing ideas and trying new approaches.
- DevOps is important for ensuring system stability before, during, and after deployment.
- AI can be applied flexibly to personalized web applications.

### Applying to Internship Work

After the event, I became more motivated to practice AWS carefully, especially in monitoring Billing, cleaning up resources, and testing systems after deployment. The DevOps and hackathon sharing also helped me better understand the importance of environment preparation, debugging, progress management, and clear result reporting.

### Results Achieved

The Meet Up helped me broaden my understanding of AWS, DevOps, hackathons, soft skills, and AI applications. I learned how to manage cloud costs, collaborate under pressure, build confidence, and prepare systems properly before real deployment.
