# Sharing and Feedback

## 7.1. Overall Reflection

The internship at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** was a valuable learning experience for me. During the internship, I not only strengthened my basic understanding of AWS but also had the opportunity to work directly with services such as **IAM, EC2, RDS, S3, CloudFormation, CloudWatch, GitHub Actions**, and practical deployment workflows.

What I appreciated most was that the program helped me understand how a cloud-based system is built, deployed, monitored, and optimized. From the first AWS labs to the **SUMMER-STORE deployment project**, I learned how to analyze issues, verify configurations, and improve the system step by step.

## 7.2. Most Satisfying Point

The most satisfying part of the internship was the support from mentors, admins, and the FCAJ community. Whenever I faced technical problems, I received clear guidance that helped me investigate and solve the issues by myself instead of only following fixed instructions.

The internship content was also highly practical, allowing me to improve both technical skills and soft skills such as self-learning, task planning, communication, and reporting.

## 7.3. What I Learned

After the internship, I gained a clearer understanding of deploying a system on AWS, from account preparation, IAM permission management, EC2/RDS/S3 setup, security configuration, system testing, and cost monitoring. I also learned how to use GitHub Actions to automate website build and deployment.

More importantly, I improved my problem-solving mindset. When an issue occurs, I learned to check each layer carefully, including service configuration, access permissions, Security Groups, resource paths, system logs, and deployment environments.

## 7.4. Suggestions

In my opinion, the program could be improved by providing a clear internship checklist at the beginning. This would help interns follow their progress more easily and understand what needs to be completed in each stage.

It would also be helpful to have short weekly review sessions or sharing sessions about common AWS lab issues. This can help interns summarize what they have learned, avoid repeated mistakes, and improve the quality of their reports.

## 7.5. Questions and Answers

**What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
The most satisfying part was having hands-on experience with AWS and receiving helpful guidance from mentors while troubleshooting real deployment issues.

**What should the program improve for future interns?**  
The program could provide a clearer progress checklist, sample materials, and short weekly review sessions so interns can track their work and consolidate knowledge more effectively.

**Would you recommend this internship to a friend? Why?**  
Yes. I would recommend this internship because it is practical, supportive, and gives students a valuable opportunity to learn cloud computing in a structured way.

**Do you have any suggestions to improve the internship experience?**  
I suggest adding an orientation session at the beginning, a collection of common troubleshooting cases, and report templates to help interns start more smoothly.

**Would you like to continue this program in the future?**  
Yes. If possible, I would like to continue learning more about DevOps, cloud security, CI/CD, and AWS system architecture.

## 7.6. Appreciation

I sincerely thank FCAJ, my mentors, and all supporting members for guiding me throughout the internship. This experience helped me become more confident with AWS, understand real deployment workflows, and gain clearer direction for developing my skills in Cloud and DevOps.
